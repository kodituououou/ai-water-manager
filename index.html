Problem → Solution

Problem

Uzbekistan uses nearly 92% of national water for irrigation. Much of that water is lost in old canals, unmetered furrows, and via inefficient schedules. Soil types and salinity vary across regions, while many farms still use fixed-time irrigation without feedback — leading to wasted water, higher costs, and environmental stress.

  • Large fraction of water used by agriculture
  • Outdated irrigation infrastructure
  • Lack of sensor-based decision-making
  • Heterogeneous soils & maintenance challenges

Solution

The proposed solution is a smart drip system that uses sensor networks, an ML controller, and zone valves to apply water precisely. By forecasting soil moisture (24–72 hours) and reacting to measured conditions, the system reduces applied water while keeping yields steady.

  • Soil moisture + temperature + flow sensing
  • LPWAN gateways and edge processing
  • ML scheduling (LSTM / GBM / hybrid)
  • Farmer dashboard for monitoring & alerts

How we'll implement the solution

Hardware & Field

  • Soil moisture sensors (capacitive) at 10–30 cm & 40–60 cm
  • Flow meters on main supply and solenoid valves per zone
  • Pressure-compensated emitters, filters and flush valves
  • LPWAN (LoRa) gateway with wired fallback

Sensor density: ~8–12 moisture sensors + 1 flow meter per hectare (adjustable by crop & layout)

Software & AI

  • Backend: FastAPI + PostgreSQL
  • Dashboard: React/Next.js with real-time updates
  • Models: LSTM & GBM ensembles predicting 24–72h moisture
  • Metrics: RMSE for forecast accuracy; % water saved vs baseline
  • Tools: PyTorch, scikit-learn, Docker, GitHub Actions

Development stages

  1. Collect sensor & weather data (2–4 weeks)
  2. Build data pipeline and baseline models (2 weeks)
  3. Prototype valve control & dashboard (2–3 weeks)
  4. Field pilot & iterative tuning (1 crop season)

Maintenance & operations

Weekly filter cleaning, quarterly sensor calibration, automated alerts for pressure/flow anomalies. Local maintenance team + simple logs will keep the system reliable.
